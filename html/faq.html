Contact Us


  1. What is 3D printing?

    3D printing uses the principles of additive manufacturing to create 3D objects out of design files. Additive manufacturing means building up from scratch rather than subtractive manufacturing which cuts away at a piece until the desired object is obtained. The printers we use lay down layers of melted plastic and build from the bottom up, using data from the files to determine when and where to extrude plastic. This creates the 3D object.


  2. What are some applications of 3D printing?

    There are endless applications to 3D printing. The most obvious is printing out parts for different systems in a cheap and efficient manner. Gears, car parts, facial reconstructions, even other 3D printers can be created. Studies have shown successful experiments in which organs were 3D-printed using a biodegradable mold and stem cells. Recently, prosthetic limbs have taken a hold in the 3D printing market. The possibilities are endless.


  3. What materials are used to print designs?

    3D printers can use several different types of materials. Thermoplastics (PLA or ABS), metal, rubber, chocolate, plaster, modelling clay, etc. The types of materials we use at NGP are ABS and PLA plastic. However, we mostly work with ABS. Jobs requested in PLA will take additional time and will include an additional charge.


  4. How thick are the layers in 3D printing?

    Each layer in a print can range from 0.1 - 0.3 millimeters, depending on what level of detail is required.


  5. What is the minimum thickness?

    We can print very thin wall thicknesses down to less than a milimeter thick, the minimum height is about 1 mm.


  6. What is the level of detail of products printed?

    Printing layers at 0.1 millimeters per layer gives us the ability to make extremely intricate designs and include very high levels of details in products.


  7. Can I print colored objects?

    It depends. Printing objects in one color is possible, but will cost extra. Printing objects in multiple colors is extremely difficult and may not be possible given our resources. If multiple colors are requested, we will see if we are able to fulfill the request to the best of our ability. This will include several additional charges (for each color, as well as the cost for chemical smoothing, which will most likely be required) and will take a much longer time.


  8. What is CAD?

    CAD stands for Computer-Aided Design. This is a technology that allows individuals to create their own designs in a program, either in 2D or 3D. The program then allows you to save that design in a file type that can be used by a 3D printer to print out your object.


  9. What types of CAD files do you accept?

    We accept STL files which are industry standard, most 3D files can be converted into STLs.


  10. How would I go about converting to the types of CAD files you accept?

    See if your design program allows you to save as STL, otherwise email us the file at danielsuh@thinkbeyondink.net and we will attempt to convert it for you.


  11. How do I go about ordering my design if I already have the file?

    Submit a request through our website. You will receive a confirmation email shortly after and another email within a few days giving you an upper-limit cost on your project. You will have 24 hours to cancel your order during this period. If you decide to cancel after, there will be an additional charge. After your print is complete, we will send you pictures of the resulting print, asking if this is satisfactory. If so, we will ship it out. If not, we will re-print at a minor charge (unless the original print is horrendous).


  12. What additional steps would I need to take if I did not know how to use CAD?

    If you lack experience in using CAD or would like someone to help you complete your design, you may contact us or submit a request through our website to commission our CAD services. This is a separate service from the 3D prints so it comes with its own costs.


  13. How long would it take to print my order?

    Most orders take under a week to process and print. Given the size or complexity of the order, this time may change.


  14. How long would it take to finish the CAD design?

    It may take us up to a week to process your CAD order. Based on the complexity of the design, it may take us a few hours to a few days to complete the design to your satisfaction.


  15. What forms of payment do you accept?

    We accept Paypal.


  16. What happens if the produced print is unsatisfactory?

    If the produced print is unsatisfactory before we ship it out, you may request a re-print at a minor charge. If you want a re-print after we have already shipped it out, you will have to submit another request for a print. This request will be separate from the initial order.


  17. Can I cancel my order?

    Yes, you may cancel your order during the period after receiving the email containing the upper-limit cost. If you decide to cancel after this period, there will be a charge varying with time. The more time after the initial period there is when you cancel, the higher the charge will be.


  18. How can I contact you for additional help?

    For any further questions, feel free to contact our CEO, Daniel Suh, at danielsuh@thinkbeyondink.net. Based on the material of your concerns, you may be referred to others within the company.


  19. How are your printing services different from other company's?

    We provide two things that other companies do not. Affordability and speed. We offer rates that add up to much cheaper custom designs that our competitors. Furthermore, while other companies can take a few weeks just to process your order, we will cut down that time by half and get you your product faster. Additionally, we provide CAD services for those inexperienced in the field, whereas many 3D printing services do not.
